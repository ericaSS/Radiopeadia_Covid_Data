Pulmonary embolism in COVID-19 positive patient AGE : 80 years, GENDER : male
Presentation : Two weeks of fever, one week of breathing difficulties.
CT : Bilateral large areas of ground-glass opacities and mild pleural effusion.
Left-sided lower lobe segmental and subsegmental pulmonary arterial filling defect.
Case Discussion : The patient was tested positive for COVID-19 and was transferred to the intensive care unit.
Many COVID-19 infected patients have coagulation marker abnormalities.
Some studies present the possibility of underdiagnosing pulmonary embolism because of the overlapping symptoms.
We have to check the d-dimer levels, and if elevated to suspect pulmonary embolism.
These patients have a poorer prognosis.
