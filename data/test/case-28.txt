COVID-19 pneumonia - AGE : 50 years, GENDER : Male
Presentation : Fever and sore throat for few days.
No history of recent travel.
CT : Multiple bilateral pulmonary ground glass opacities are seen, more predominant at lower lung lobes.
Case Discussion : This male patient presented with fever and upper respiratory tract symptoms to ER.
Clinical triaging was suspicious for COVID-19 pneumonia.
The patient underwent CT scan which revealed bilateral pulmonary ground glass opacities that are commonly seen with COVID-19 pneumonia, that was subsequently confirmed by a nasopharyngeal swab.
